Ethnography

Ethnographic Fallacy

  • Too often -- not always -- ethnography suffers from a myopia that sharply delineates the behavior at close range but obscures the less visible structures and processes that engender and sustain the behavior. - Stephen Steinberg
  • Details vs. constraints and opportunities.
  • Linking micro with macro scales

Extended Place Method

  • Focus is on fieldwork site, but then moved beyond to include other connected spaces and institutions.

Interviewing

Quantitative vs. Qualitative Interviews

QuantitativeQualitative
Criteria Emphasizedreliability and validitygenerality in theory formation
Point of Viewinterviewer'sinterviewee's
Tangentsdiscouragedencouraged
Formatstructuredsemi-structured or un-structured

Interview Planning and Guides

  • areas to be covered and background research
  • notion of order and flow
  • thoughts on question formulation and language
  • consideration of setting
  • consideration of social relationships
  • structured sections for basic facts, informed consent, and closing
  • practice / pilot interviews

Questions

  • Introduction
  • Follow-up
  • Probing
  • Specifying
  • Direct
  • Indirect
  • Structuring
  • Silence
  • Interpreting

Interview Phases

  • Introduction: breaking ice, getting informed consent, gaining trust
  • Gathering basic information
  • Body
  • Closing: letting interviewee ask questions or raise concerns

Tips

  • Be knowledgeable
  • Use some structure
  • Be clear
  • Be gentle
  • Be sensitive
  • Be open and adjust to interviewee
  • Be critical
  • Remember what has been said
  • Interpret meanings without imposing
  • Balance between talking too much or too little
  • Be sensitive to ethical issues
